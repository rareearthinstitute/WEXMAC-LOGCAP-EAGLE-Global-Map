Strategic Framing — Administration Posture
For too long, leaders in Washington abandoned the simple wisdom of the Monroe Doctrine. Business as usual will not stand.
— SecDef Pete Hegseth, Americas Counter Cartel Conference, Doral FL, March 2026
Our ability to project, employ, and sustain forces to secure key terrain depends on a robust and resilient logistics enterprise. The dispersed geography, partner nation dependencies, and limited infrastructure across the USSOUTHCOM AOR create sustainment vulnerabilities that demand deliberate, coordinated efforts to mitigate.
— Gen. Francis Donovan, SOUTHCOM Commander, Senate SASC Posture Statement, March 2026
⚑
"Donroe Doctrine" — Monroe Doctrine Reimagined
The Trump administration's 2025 NSS and 2026 NDS frame the Western Hemisphere as homeland defense space. SOUTHCOM is directed to "defend the homeland at range, speed, and scale" — a departure from the traditional security cooperation framing. Hegseth invoked the Monroe Doctrine as explicit legal basis for military counter-narcotics operations.
⟳
NORTHCOM-SOUTHCOM Merger Under Discussion
The Pentagon and White House are actively examining merging SOUTHCOM with NORTHCOM into a single "AMERICOM" covering all of North, Central, and South America. Hegseth has expressed interest in reducing combatant command count. If implemented, it would reshape theater logistics contracting structures.
!
USAID Dismantled — DoD Filling the Gap
USAID was effectively eliminated in early 2025. Civilian HA/DR coordination, country capacity-building, and development programming have been defunded across all SOUTHCOM partner nations. DoD contractor vehicles — primarily WEXMAC — are now the default instrument for humanitarian response and capacity-building in the AOR.

Operation Southern Spear — Active Campaign
Nov 2025 – present
Command structure
JTF Southern Spear / II MEF
Announced Oct 2025 by Hegseth, Southern Spear is led by II Marine Expeditionary Force (Camp Lejeune), synchronizing counter-narcotics operations across the Western Hemisphere. Employs naval task forces, UAS, and autonomous platforms for maritime interdiction.
Scope of operations
Caribbean + Eastern Pacific Maritime Corridor
Over 45 strikes against suspected smuggling vessels and 150+ reported deaths through early 2026. USCG Maritime Security Response Teams accompanied by USMC Special Purpose Forces conduct maritime interdiction. Shadow oil fleet targeting added as secondary mission.
Ecuador ground operations
March 2026 — Joint Ecuadorian-US Operations
USSOUTHCOM provided intelligence enabling capture of "Fito," Ecuador's most wanted FTO leader. March 3, 2026: joint Ecuadorian-US military operations against Los Lobos cartel in Guayaquil. DEA Guayaquil Resident Office and DEA Europe Division both participated.

Forward Demand Signals — Logistics Contract Implications
Critical — Haiti mass migration risk
HA/DR Mass Casualty Response
Donovan explicitly flagged Haiti's FTO-driven instability as risking a "maritime mass migration event." A major Haiti crisis would be the largest short-fuse HA/DR mobilization in the AOR since the 2010 earthquake. WEXMAC is the only vehicle fast enough to respond at scale. No USAID to coordinate — DoD contractors are the first line.
High — Venezuela recovery/reconstruction
Post-Maduro Economic Recovery Access
Rubio's three-phase plan explicitly requires Western and American companies to have "access to the Venezuelan market." Phase 2 (recovery) needs logistics infrastructure, supply chain, and reconstruction capacity. With USAID gone, DoD contract vehicles — WEXMAC, LOGCAP V — become the instruments for both military and humanitarian engagement during transition.
High — Panama maritime logistics
Canal Security + New Site Infrastructure
Three new US military sites in Panama, USACE construction at Cristóbal Colón, and the explicit priority of "ensuring unfettered US access to the Panama Canal" create sustained logistics demand at a chokepoint with direct relevance to INDOPACOM maritime prepositioning. RMGS's international shipping company relationship covers Pacific, SE Asia, NE Asia, and island chains — this is the strategic bridge.
Elevated — counter-FTO logistics support
Op. Southern Spear Sustainment
The II MEF Joint Task Force requires sustained logistics support across a dispersed maritime operating area. Donovan flagged the AOR's "limited infrastructure" and "partner nation dependencies" as vulnerabilities requiring deliberate mitigation. As OSS operations shift FTO networks to new methods, the operational tempo and footprint requirements will increase, not decrease.
